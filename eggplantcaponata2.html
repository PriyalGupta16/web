PROCEDURES

  • Place diced eggplant in a colander, sprinkle with salt, and let it sit for about 30 minutes. Rinse and pat dry with paper towels.
  • In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the
    diced eggplant and sauté until golden brown and softened. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  • In the same skillet, add chopped onion, diced celery, and
    diced red bell pepper. Sauté until the vegetables are softened.
  • Add minced garlic and sauté for an additional minute until fragrant.
  • Stir in crushed tomatoes, red wine vinegar, capers, sliced green olives, and the sautéed eggplant. Cook for about 15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  • Toast pine nuts in a dry pan over medium heat until golden brown.
  • Remove the skillet from heat and stir in toasted
    pine nuts and chopped fresh basil.
  • Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  • Let the Eggplant Caponata cool to room
    temperature before serving.
  • Serve as a topping for crostini, as
    a side dish, or as a condiment. Enjoy!
