PROCEDURES

  • Glera Grapes: Use Glera grapes (formerly known as Prosecco grapes) as the primary grape variety.
  • Harvesting: Pick Glera grapes at the right ripeness, usually in early autumn.
  • Soft Pressing: Softly press the grapes to obtain the must (grape juice).
  • Fermentation: Allow the fermentation of the must in stainless steel tanks under controlled temperatures.
  • Second Fermentation (Charmat Method): Transfer the base wine to pressurized tanks for a secondary fermentation, which creates the bubbles in Prosecco.
  • Aging (optional): Some Proseccos undergo a short period of aging in tanks to develop their flavors.
  • Clarification: Filter the wine to remove sediment and solids.
  • Bottling: Bottle the wine under pressure to retain its natural effervescence.
  • Labeling: Label the bottles as Prosecco DOC or Prosecco DOCG according to the appellation.
